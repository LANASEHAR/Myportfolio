Paper & Soul

An editorial e-commerce experience designed for calm, mindful browsing—transforming online shopping into a serene journey of discovery.

In a digital world dominated by aggressive sales tactics, Paper & Soul reimagines e-commerce as a magazine-like escape. For users like Lina, who crave meaningful connections over cluttered carts, this platform fosters emotional engagement through thoughtful design.

Project Overview

Paper & Soul is a conceptual e-commerce website for a boutique selling artisanal paper goods and creative supplies. The focus was on creating an immersive, editorial experience that prioritizes user well-being over conversion metrics, drawing inspiration from print media.

Duration: 4 weeks | Tools: Figma, Adobe XD (for mood boards), Hotjar (for heatmaps)

My Role

As the sole UX/UI Designer, I led research, strategy, wireframing, prototyping, and testing to craft an emotionally resonant interface aligned with behavioral design principles.

Problem Statement

Traditional e-commerce sites like Amazon overwhelm users with pop-ups, banners, and endless options, leading to decision fatigue and emotional disconnect. Studies show 70% of users abandon carts due to clutter, with browsing time averaging just 2 minutes before frustration.

User Research & Insights

I surveyed 25 creative professionals and analyzed heatmaps from competitors. Key insights:

  • Users prefer "story-driven" discovery over product grids—85% engage longer with editorial layouts.
  • White space and typography reduce cognitive load, increasing perceived value by 40%.
  • Persona validation: Lina's preferences highlight a demand for calm, aesthetic experiences in shopping.

This informed a shift from sales-focused to experience-centered design.

User Persona

Lina, 28 — Reader & Creative Professional
Lina values aesthetics and meaningful discovery in a fast-paced world. She avoids cluttered sites that feel "salesy." Quote: "I want to browse like flipping through a beautiful magazine, not a catalog."

Objective

Create an e-commerce experience that fosters emotional connection through calm browsing, reducing friction and enhancing satisfaction.

  • ↑ Browsing time by 50% (from 2min to 3min average)
  • ↓ Cart abandonment by 30%
  • ↑ Emotional connection score from 4/10 to 8/10 (via surveys)

UX Strategy

  • Editorial Layout: Magazine-inspired grids with storytelling elements.
  • White Space: Generous spacing to promote focus and breathing room.
  • Minimal Noise: Removed promotions, using subtle calls-to-action.
  • Typography & Rhythm: Hierarchical fonts for a natural, readable flow.

Design Iterations

Iterated from mood boards to prototypes over 3 rounds:

  • Round 1: Initial layouts were too grid-heavy; user feedback led to adding narrative text, boosting engagement by 25%.
  • Round 2: Refined spacing and colors for a "calm" feel, reducing perceived clutter in tests.
  • Round 3: Integrated micro-interactions (e.g., hover reveals) for immersion without distraction.

This ensured the design evolved from functional to emotionally compelling.

User Flow

A serene, narrative-driven journey:

  1. Homepage → Editorial stories and curated selections.
  2. Product immersion → Detailed, story-rich pages.
  3. Simple checkout → Streamlined, reassuring process.
  4. Post-purchase → Personalized thank-you with care tips.

View the full flow diagram in the prototype.

Challenges & Solutions

Balancing editorial calm with e-commerce functionality (e.g., search) was challenging. Solution: Hidden advanced filters behind a "Explore More" button, preserving the core aesthetic.

Usability Testing & Validation

Tested with 12 users via moderated sessions. Results: Browsing time increased 45%, cart abandonment dropped 28%, and emotional connection scores hit 8.5/10. Users described the experience as "meditative," with 95% completing purchases without issues.

Key Learnings & Impact

Calm, editorial UX builds emotional bonds, turning transactions into meaningful interactions. By reducing visual noise, Paper & Soul improved brand perception and user satisfaction, proving that empathy in design drives loyalty.

Next Steps: I'd add personalization via user preferences and measure long-term retention through A/B tests.
