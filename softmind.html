SoftMind

Mental health tracking app designed to support emotional awareness and reduce daily stress through calm, non-intrusive interactions.

View Figma Prototype

Project Overview

SoftMind is a non-clinical mental health tracking app created as a UX concept project. The goal was to help users recognize emotional patterns, reduce stress, and build emotional awareness without overwhelming them with data, dashboards, or complex interactions.

My Role

I worked as the sole Product Designer, responsible for user research synthesis, UX strategy, interaction design, UI decisions, and high-fidelity prototyping.

Problem Statement

Many mental health apps unintentionally increase cognitive load by presenting dense dashboards, frequent tracking prompts, and excessive data visualization. For users experiencing anxiety or decision fatigue, this complexity can create additional stress instead of relief.

User Persona

Adam — 30 years old
Office employee experiencing daily anxiety spikes, mental fatigue, and difficulty making decisions after long workdays.

UX Objective

Reduce decision fatigue and emotional friction by designing a predictable, reassuring experience that supports emotional regulation without demanding excessive user effort.

UX Strategy

  • Use a calm color palette with soft contrast to avoid overstimulation
  • Limit text and choices per screen
  • Map emotional states clearly without clinical terminology
  • Maintain predictable navigation to reinforce a sense of safety

User Flow

1. User opens the app during a moment of stress
2. Immediate emotional check-in with minimal input
3. Guided calming action (breathing, reflection, pause)
4. Gentle positive reinforcement
5. Exit the app without cognitive fatigue

Key Design Decisions

  • Soft, warm color palette to promote emotional safety
  • Rounded shapes and spacing to reduce visual tension
  • Short, supportive microcopy instead of instructional text
  • No dashboards, scores, or productivity metrics

Results & Key Learnings

This concept demonstrates how reducing cognitive load and emotional friction can create a sense of calm and safety for users. The project reinforced the idea that UX design can act as a form of emotional regulation, not just an interface for data interaction.

What I Would Improve Next

With more time and user testing, I would validate emotional check-in patterns, test different reinforcement messages, and explore lightweight personalization while maintaining the app’s calm and non-intrusive nature.
